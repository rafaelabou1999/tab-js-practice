Bill Gates

(1955-today)

Bill Gates is an American entrepreneur, software developer, investor, and philanthropist. He co-founded Microsoft Corporation, the world's largest software company, with Paul Allen in 1975. Gates led the company to become one of the most successful tech companies in history before retiring in 2008. He is known for his philanthropic donations through the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation, which focuses on reducing global poverty and improving healthcare. Gates is also recognized as one of the wealthiest people in the world, with a net worth of over $130 billion as of 2021.

Charles Babbage, The father of the Computer

(1791-1871)

Charles Babbage, often referred to as the "father of the computer," was a prominent English mathematician, philosopher, and inventor. Born on December 26, 1791, Babbage is best known for his contributions to the field of computing and his design of the Analytical Engine. Babbage's fascination with machines and mathematics led him to envision a mechanical device that could perform complex calculations automatically. In 1822, he proposed the concept of a Difference Engine - a machine designed to calculate polynomial functions. Although never fully completed during his lifetime due to funding issues, Babbage's ideas laid the foundation for modern computers.

Steve Jobs

(1955-2011)

Steve Jobs, widely recognized as a visionary and innovator, was the co-founder of Apple Inc. and played a pivotal role in revolutionizing the technology industry. With his charismatic personality and relentless pursuit of perfection, Jobs left an indelible mark on the world. Born on February 24, 1955, in San Francisco, California, Jobs grew up with a passion for electronics and computers. In 1976, he co-founded Apple alongside Steve Wozniak in his parents' garage. Together, they developed the Apple I computer - a milestone that marked the beginning of their extraordinary journey. Under Jobs' leadership as CEO from 1997 to 2011 (with a brief hiatus), Apple transformed into one of the most valuable and influential companies globally. His ability to envision groundbreaking products such as the Macintosh computer, iPod, iPhone, and iPad propelled Apple to unprecedented success.

Ada Lovelace

(1815-1852)

Ada Lovelace (1815-1852) was a British mathematician and writer, known for her work on Charles Babbage's early mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine. Lovelace is credited with writing the first algorithm intended to be processed by a machine, and thus is considered to be the world's first computer programmer. She also recognized the potential for computers to go beyond simple calculation and be used for more complex tasks, such as creating music and art. Lovelace's contributions to computing have earned her recognition as a pioneer in the field, and she is celebrated each year on Ada Lovelace Day, an international celebration of women in science, technology, engineering, and maths (STEM) fields.

Alan Turing

(1912-1954)

Alan Turing was a British mathematician, logician, and computer scientist. He is widely considered to be the father of modern computing and artificial intelligence. During World War II, he worked for the British government's code-breaking operation at Bletchley Park, where he made significant contributions to cracking the Nazi Enigma code. After the war, he continued to work in the field of computing, developing the first electronic computer in the UK. Tragically, Turing was convicted of homosexuality, which was illegal at the time, and forced to undergo chemical castration. He died by suicide in 1954 at the age of 41.

John von Neumann

(1903-1957)

John von Neumann was a Hungarian-American mathematician who made groundbreaking contributions to a wide range of fields, including mathematics, physics, economics, and computer science. He is often credited with helping to invent the modern computer architecture and played a key role in the development of game theory. He also served as a consultant on various military projects, including the Manhattan Project, which led to the creation of the atomic bomb.
